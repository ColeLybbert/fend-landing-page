Why Do We Sleep?

    Helps brain function

    One of the ways sleeping can help your body and mind is improving brain function. Sleep helps with long-term memory, it strengthens neural connections and also gets rid of unwanted neural connections. Sleeping will also imporve your ablility to learn something new. Some of the symptoms of you brain reorganizing itself during sleep is headaches, high blood pressure, irregular heat beat, feeling tired even after sleep, dry mouth, and weight gain.

    Reduces disease risk

    One of the ways sleeping can help your body and mind is reducing your chance for diseases. During sleep you brain will keep your heart and blood vessels healthy, if you do not get enough sleep it can result in high blood pressure, lowered heart function, and heart disease. Along with a proper diet and lifestyle changes, sleep can help you prevent chronic diseases. Eating healthy foods and exercising as well as not smoking tobacco and drinking alchohol.

    Creates memories

    One of the ways sleeping can help your body and mind is be creating new memories and solidifying old ones. Sleep results in changes in the brain the helps solidify memories, as mentioned before is also helps strengthen neural connections which helps the brain remember experiences. During sleep your body changes synapses and neurotransmitters to form larger networks to store all of your memories depending on the strength of these connections in certain areas of the brain.

    Repair and Restoration

    One of the best ways sleep helps both your mind and body is reparing and restoring you body. When you sleep your body releases proteins and hormones the repairs damages to your body like your skin or muscles. It's important to get plenty of sleep if your trying to build muscle. Your brain also flushes out the toxic metabolites and repairs our DNA sharpening our memory and improves decision making skills and problem solving.
